INSTRUMENTATION   >>

- 6 very large and resonant Chimes:
   Wood (hard, avoid the bamboo),
   Bamboo (medium high*) * For instance the KOLBERG Bamboo wind-chimes, medium "Naruko", only the highest 28 tubes.,
   Metal (cans for instance, organized by low, medium and high ranges, avoid the typically used scale-organized Wind chimes racks),
   Stone (may be replaced by additional irregular chimes if necessary),
   Glass (lamp bulbs for instance, organized by low, medium and high ranges, avoid the typically used scale-organized Wind chimes racks),
   Seashell (including very thin, smooth and delicate range as well as medium and lower one).
   ALL must resonate long.
  
- 5 Temple blocks

- 8 Cymbals: Hi-hat (with Pedal set on half open), 1 Orchestra heavy cymbal, 22" (inches) cymbal, 18" cymbal, 16" cymbal, 16" Rived cymbal, 12" Splash cymbal and 10" Splash cymbal.
   These dimensions represent the suggestion of a scale and may be adapted freely.

- 3 Tam-tams: very high, med. high and (med.) low

- 7 Skins or Drums for example, consisting of; 3 Bongos (very high, high and medium**), 3 Tom-toms (high, medium and (very) low) and Bass drum (particularly resonant).
    This instrument distribution represents the suggestion of a scale and may be adapted freely.
    ** The medium one may be replaced with a Chinese drum "Low Pitch Battle Drum" for instance.

PROGRAM NOTE
Purple is a general term for the range of shades of colour occurring between red and blue. If we consider the red as the human passion and the blue a mysticism-oriented colour then the purple position in between would be (ideally) the magic gate in between. Through a high level of excitement and virtuosity I wanted this piece to spread the huge amount of energy, vibrations and wishes raised by the performer.
Would it be (musically) possible to pass "through the door" ?

COMMENTS  
Warning ! Although it is not explicitly wished to break glass bulbs during the performance, so many bulbs were broken during the rehearsals that it is absolutely part of the piece characteristics now. Ya-Hsin CHENG made it in such spectacular and cute way during her preliminary round performance for the Geneva Competition 2009 that it quickly appeared an opportunity to show it instead of hide it. Aesthetically it corresponds to Alin Gherman's (permanent) experimental approach.
Moreover, the specific noise produced by the breaking glass could be considered as a prolongation of the simultaneously played cymbal.   
(Conclusion : You may break glass bulbs BUT ONLY if the glass chimes were NOT made by the composer ! Go on !)

の日本語

「紫」は赤と青の中間色をさす。
赤が人間の情熱的な感情をあらわす色、青が神秘的な色だとすると、紫はその間の、魔法の橋のような位置にある（理想的には）。
この曲では非常に大きなエネルギー、バイブレーション、そして希求する心を発信するように、演奏者には高いレベルでのパワーの持続と音楽的テクニックが要求される。
音楽的な意味で、「ドアを通り抜ける」ことは可能だろうか？
ジュネーブ国際音楽コンクール２００９とPROヘルヴェティア、スイス文化財団により委嘱。エディシオン・パピヨン社（スイス、ジュネーブ）出版。
２つの独立した部分（それのみで演奏可能）は、第一部と第三部がもとになっていて、「紫の儀式」から抜粋したものである。
——シンバル・ヴィタミン（第一部の長いバージョン）
——ダンス、　今にも降る雨の下で（第三部のみ）
アリン・ゲルマン

PROGRAM NOTE
As the title says, the piece shows a thematic struggle between the two hands. The question is: Why the struggle ends in such way ?
This piece is based on a bass line taken from the piece : "Nyolc Zongoradarab" op.3 by György KURTAG.

COMMENTS
Big hands are required in order to play some of the very large intervals like the 9th and 10th (B natural - D natural).

5 Miniatures  >>

1. Frati de cruce    (Transylvania - Sibiu)  (frères de croix - blood brothers)
2. L'epine    (Dobrogea, the Black Sea sunny region)   (gimpele - the thorn-bush),
3. Autre danse valaque a deux    (Walachia)  (alt dans valah în doi - another  walachian dance for two)
4. Gheorghita    (Walachia - Muntenia)
5. Bocet     (Oas county - Transylvania) (lamentations funeraires - funeral lamentations) 


PROGRAM NOTE
Inspired by 5 Romanian folk songs. As the Romanian composer Dan DEDIU said after listening to "Folclor" : "The nostalgia of simplicity trough a (contemporary) musical vocabulary which is not innocent." (in Romanian)-- "Nostalgia naivitatii "vazuta" printr-un vocabular muzical (contemporan) neinocent. Dan DEDIU, Breaza, 2004).
The miniatures are based on folkloric melodies from the "Folclor muzical" anthology by Emilia COMISEL, Education Ministry, Didactic and pedagogical Edition, Bucharest, 1967.
