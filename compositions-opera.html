Program Note

This composition is based on the famous metaphysical poetry "Luceafărul" by the Romanian poet Mihai EMINESCU. The excerpt used in this composition was translated by C.M. Popescu "Lucifer".

(…)

Lucifer set out and o'er
The sky his wings extended,
And million years flew past before
As many moments ended.


A sky of stars above his way,
A sky of stars below,
As lightning flash midst them astray
In one continuous flow.


Till round his primal chaos hurled
When from enwrapping night
The first, upflaming dawn unfurled
Its miracle of light.


Still further flew he ere the start
Of things of form devoid,
Spurred by the yearning of his heart,
Far back into the void.


Yet where he reach'd is not the bourn
Nor yet where eye can see,
Beyond where struggling time was torn
Out of eternity.


Around him there was naught. And still,
Strange yearning there was yet,
A yearning that the space did fill,
As when the blind forget.


(…)

Take back this halo from my head,
Take back my starry lour,
And give to me, O God, instead
Of human love one hour.


(…)

Translation by C.M. Popescu "Lucifer"

Comments

"The world has become transparent — a shattered mirror where our real lives are only displayed in fragmented glimpses — we seek out authenticity, intimacy and grand emotions — we have gadgets and filters to assist us in constantly seizing, accentuating and sharing the authentic and real parts of our lives. At the same time we have lost our perspective. Reality quickly changes, erodes, and leaves us — alone — with an overwhelming powerlessness and in a state of free fall."

Performances

Premiere — September 2014, Teater Katapult, Aarhus, Denmark, in the frame of OPERATIC ACTIONS: LUCIFER, ENLIGHTEN WITHOUT BLINDING, as part of a Grand Opera work in progress by Ensemble Musiques Nouvelles (BE). Laurent Houque (violin), Jean-Pol Zanutel (cello), Pierre Quiriny (percussions), Charles Michiels (clarinet) & André Ristic (piano), Christian Tscherning Larsen (trombone), Daniel Carlsson (countertenor), Elise Gäbele (soprano).

2015 — June 6th, Arsenic Hall, Mons, Belgium. In the frame of Mons 2015 European Capital of Culture by Ensemble Musiques Nouvelles (BE). Laurent Houque (violin), Jean-Pol Zanutel (cello), Pierre Quiriny (percussions), Charles Michiels (clarinet) & André Ristic (piano), Christian Tscherning Larsen (trombone), Daniel Carlsson (countertenor), Camilla Løvstad (soprano).
