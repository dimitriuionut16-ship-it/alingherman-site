Program Note

Purple is a general term for the range of shades of colour occurring between red and blue. If we consider the red as the human passion and the blue a mysticism-oriented colour then the purple position in between would be (ideally) the magic gate in between. Through a high level of excitement and virtuosity I wanted this piece to spread the huge amount of energy, vibrations and wishes raised by the performer.

Would it be (musically) possible to pass “through the door”?
「紫」は赤と青の中間色をさす。赤が人間の情熱的な感情をあらわす色、青が神秘的な色だとすると、紫はその間の、魔法の橋のような位置にある（理想的には）。この曲では非常に大きなエネルギー、バイブレーション、そして希求する心を発信するように、演奏者には高いレベルでのパワーの持続と音楽的テクニックが要求される。

音楽的な意味で、「ドアを通り抜ける」ことは可能だろうか？

Instrumentation

- 6 very large and resonant Chimes:
  Wood (hard, avoid the bamboo),
  Bamboo (medium high*) * For instance the KOLBERG Bamboo wind-chimes, medium “Naruko”, only the highest 28 tubes,
  Metal (cans for instance, organized by low, medium and high ranges, avoid scale-organized Wind chimes racks),
  Stone (may be replaced by additional irregular chimes if necessary),
  Glass (lamp bulbs for instance, organized by low, medium and high ranges, avoid scale-organized Wind chimes racks),
  Seashell (including very thin, smooth and delicate range as well as medium and lower one).
  ALL must resonate long.

- 5 Temple blocks

- 8 Cymbals: Hi-hat (Pedal set on half open), 1 Orchestra heavy cymbal, 22″ cymbal, 18″ cymbal, 16″ cymbal, 16″ Rived cymbal, 12″ Splash cymbal and 10″ Splash cymbal.
These dimensions represent the suggestion of a scale and may be adapted freely.

- 3 Tam-tams: very high, med. high and (med.) low

- 7 Skins or Drums for example, consisting of: 3 Bongos (very high, high and medium**), 3 Tom-toms (high, medium and (very) low) and Bass drum (particularly resonant).
This instrument distribution represents the suggestion of a scale and may be adapted freely.
** The medium one may be replaced with a Chinese drum “Low Pitch Battle Drum” for instance.

accessories (optional)

mallets
- 1 Tam-tam beater
- 2 Snare drum sticks
- 1 metal brush

Comments

Although it is not explicitly wished to break glass bulbs during the performance, so many bulbs were broken during rehearsals that it is now part of the piece’s characteristics. Ya-Hsin CHENG did it in such a spectacular way during her preliminary round at the Geneva Competition 2009 that it became an opportunity to show rather than hide it.

(Conclusion: You may break glass bulbs BUT ONLY if the glass chimes were NOT made by the composer!)

Program Note

As the title says, the piece shows a thematic struggle between the two hands. The question is: Why does the struggle end in such a way?

This piece is based on a bass line taken from “Nyolc Zongoradarab” op.3 by György KURTÁG.

Comments

Big hands are required in order to play some of the very large intervals like the 9th and 10th (B natural – D natural).

3. Autre danse valaque à deux  (Walachia) — alt dans valah în doi, another walachian dance for two
4. Gheorghițã  (Walachia — Muntenia)
5. Bocet  (Oaș county — Transylvania) — lamentations funéraires, funeral lamentations

Program Note

Inspired by 5 Romanian folk songs. As the Romanian composer Dan DEDIU said after listening to “Folclor”: “The nostalgia of simplicity through a (contemporary) musical vocabulary which is not innocent.” (in Romanian — “Nostalgia naivitatii „vazuta” printr-un vocabular muzical (contemporan) neinocent.” — Dan DEDIU, Breaza, 2004)

The miniatures are based on folkloric melodies from the “Folclor muzical” anthology by Emilia COMISEL, Education Ministry, Didactic and Pedagogical Edition, Bucharest, 1967.

Miscellaneous

A piano with 3 pedals (Una Corda, Sostenuto, Sustain) is required to play the piece.
Current version: v.3 / revised 2008 (color). Ask for a free update if you have older versions!
