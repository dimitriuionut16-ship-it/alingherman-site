- Le Samouraï et la geisha"   (opus 43, 2000)    duration: ca.  4'50"   Excerpt
(mp3 file - 58"of 4'49"- the beginning - piano: Alin GHERMAN, "live concert" at the Royal Conservatory of Music of Brussels - 31th March 2001)
score(view  and play yourself - first page only ! ...for demonstration purpose - PDF file - printing allowed)
Introduction in english   -  introduction en français    -  introducere în româneste
Japanese Kabuki theatre, Nippon tradition, inspired this celebrated modern piece. Exaggeration and expressive force are the distinctive traits of this flamboyant theatre genre, first making its appearance in the 17th century. The word Kabuki means, « to lose balance ». In this theatre of frivolity, the actors do not  sing, but they do everything else, i.e. dance, love, war. The spirit of the poetic moments succeeds against the fighting and hurting everything in this lavish décor.

"Edouard Bastin" 2001 COMPOSITION PRIZE Ciney - Belgium.
