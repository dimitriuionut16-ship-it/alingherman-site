Video Score PREVIEW Score (locked PDF)

Program Note

This piece is a tribute to the early 20th century’s mechanical piano. A movement which departs from the extremes, leading to a chaos that results in a strong mechanical pattern that becomes necessary to tease. The question — How would that music become human again? — represents one of the challenges of the piece. The revised version of 2010 offers an exciting development of the polyrhythmic patterns already presented in the short version (v.1, 2007).

About the Bösendorfer CEUS Recording

The rhythmic complexity and extreme virtuosity required by Klavierstück made a mechanical piano of the highest calibre the ideal solution. The Bösendorfer Imperial CEUS offers attack precision that transcends standard MIDI, resulting in a rhythmic richness and unexpected sonorities faithfully reproduced at every playback. The CEUS-controlled recording blurs the boundary between mechanical and human performance.

Program Note

“Serious joke!” — This group of three pieces was conceived as a preparatory path to semi-improvisation. They may be played separately and without a page-turner.

“Lamentation” (“L’épine”, excerpt of “folclor”) serves as an introductory atmosphere to make performers and audience more sensitive to the contemplation of minimal note usage.

“Other spaces” invites the pianists to recompose the atmosphere of given notes, preparing missing parameters in advance.

Prepared piano and percussion

Haute tension Beijing 2011
opus 752008ca. 6′30"Excerpt
+
Video Score PREVIEW Score (locked PDF)

Program Note

“Haute tension” (= High tension) was composed during a tensional period. I wanted to transpose this potential energy onto percussive instruments and search for a certain beauty and interest in the “danger”. The situation of discomfort gives a special “alert” power and the outburst remains uncertain. The visual aspect of the musicians being under pressure adds a second dramatic layer — the music is made by visible tensions and sounds.

Comments

As shown in the video clip, the musicians play with a CLICK (headphones) and therefore have no visual contact. This technical parameter makes the musicians even more alert, which is interesting for the audience to watch.

Program Note

“O poveste” means “A story” in Romanian. It’s an epic journey with elements of imaginative tales. Lyrical, elastic melodies are placed around contrasting timbral densities. The “lighting” of elements is mainly focused; the rest remain murky but still real. Sometimes the trail leads to brief meditative moments, and eventually to ether…

Program Note

“Mouths & Strings — lale” is a surprise box with crazy dolls inside. Their expression is achieved by musical and theatrical means. Shake it and open! Performers and listeners must both “find a way” to the sense of these rushing minutes.

“Lale” means “dolls” in Polish and is intended to be the first of a collection of express string quartets.
